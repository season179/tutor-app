Coach Echo · Tutoring Workflow

Withholding the answer, on purpose.

The model has the answer instantly. The whole product is the discipline around it — a fixed process that makes a child understand, attempt, and explain before anything gets solved. This is that process — what a learner moves through for one homework problem, the rules that never bend, and the system that enforces them.

Invariants

The rules the system enforces in every step — never left to the model’s discretion.
✓

Never hand over the path

No full solution and no final answer before the child has done the cognitive work.

✓

The comprehension gate is hard

Solving stays locked until the child shows they understand the question — but how they prove it flexes with age and mood.

✓

One demand per turn

Ask exactly one thing, then stop and wait. No running ahead of the student.

✓

A separate judge of “correct”

Correctness comes from a verifier, only on checkable claims. Open reasoning is judged by engagement, so exploring is never marked wrong.

✓

Mistakes are information

Praise the process, never humiliate, and treat “I don’t know” as a useful signal — not a failure.

✓

“Just tell me” → reframed

A demand for the answer is redirected into framing the task, never refused coldly.

✓

Speak the child's languages

Match the task's language for subject terms and required output; mirror the child's comfortable language — Malay, Chinese, or English — for rapport and scaffolding; default to English when uncertain; treat code-switching (Manglish / rojak) as normal, not a mistake. See Languages →

The steps

Numbered because order carries meaning. Green = where the discipline is enforced.
0
Once per session · human before method

Session open

Greet by name, recall one relevant thing from last time (“last time, regrouping tripped you up”), normalize mistakes, and set one goal. If behavior spikes later, do a reset — return to something they can do — not another warm-up.

1
Per problem

Capture & parse

The child shares the problem by image, voice, or text. Extraction reads the text, problem type, givens, the unknown, any diagram, and a confidence score. On low confidence, ask the child to confirm or read it aloud rather than building on a bad parse.

2
Per problem · the gate

Frame the task enforced

Before any solving, the child identifies what success looks like. This is the general form of “identify the question” — and the check branches by problem type.

Problem typeFraming check
Word problemThe Three Reads — (1) what’s the situation? (2) what quantities, and how related? (3) what exactly are we asked to find?
Equation“What are we solving for? What does the = tell us? What will a finished answer look like?”
Geometry / diagram“What are we finding? What facts are given? Which parts of the diagram matter?”
Computation“What operation or form is asked? Estimate — what kind of answer makes sense?”

Pass condition: the child produces or selects a valid “We need to find ___.” The gate never opens without participation — but the form of proof adapts:

Child’s stateHow they may pass
Anxious / shut downChoose between two restatements, fill one blank, point/tap, or co-restate with the tutor.
SteadyState the “We need to find ___” sentence in their own words.
RushingThe stricter version — full restatement plus “show me why that’s what it’s asking.”

2b · conditional — Activate prior knowledge only when framing reveals a missing tool, memory flags a relevant past misconception, or the child is stuck and needs a bridge. Otherwise skip straight to action; stacking gates is what triggers shutdown.

3
Per problem

Plan only the first step

Elicit the first tiny step — “what’s the very first step, not the answer?” Full-path planning is forbidden. If the child can’t propose one, the tutor models a single micro-step (“I’ll start the first move, then you take the next”), then hands control back.

4
Per problem · the heart · repeats per step

The step loop enforced

Elicit→ Wait→ Scaffold→ Act→ Verify→ Explain→ Feedback→ Fade→ Advance

↻ repeats per step · advance only when the step is correct (where checkable) and the child shows enough reasoning for the phase

Wait is an adaptive timer, not a flat two minutes — it tells active thinking from stuck silence:

Age bandHint threshold (unproductive silence)
7–8~20–45 seconds
9–12~45–90 seconds
Hard ceiling (all)never exceed ~2 minutes without a small hint

Extend while the child is verbalizing useful reasoning; shorten — and make the hint smaller — when anxious or shutting down.

The support ladder has a floor and an exit, so the loop always terminates and never traps a child:

Floor

Deepest support is tutor models the step, then the child redoes that same micro-step. A hint is never just the bare answer.

Park-it

If still blocked after the modeled redo, park it — mark the misconception, move on or wrap, and revisit next session. No grinding the child down.

5
Per problem

Answer check — against the framed task

Return to the “We need to find ___” blank from step 2: check units, give a sentence answer, sanity-check the earlier estimate, or substitute back — whichever fits the type. This closes the loop right back to comprehension.

6
Per problem

Remember it

One short reflection — “what helped you figure it out?” — then persist the skill practiced, support level needed, any misconception, the behavior signal, and the scaffold that worked. The homework problem is done here. No second problem is forced.

7
Session / skill level · near close

Transfer check enforced

Solving a homework problem with help is evidence, not proof. Near the end, for a skill that’s a candidate for mastery, the child attempts one independent, reduced-help item.

Passing it is what promotes mastery. Solving the original with heavy support leaves the skill at “developing.” Skipped if the child is dysregulated or out of time — mastery just stays put. This keeps the transfer guarantee without making every problem spawn extra homework.

8
Once per session

Wrap-up

The child says what they learned; the tutor assigns one small, specific practice task and previews next time. The system writes the session summary plus mastery and misconception deltas — which become the input to step 0 next time. That loop is how “the tutor remembers what failed before” actually happens.

Languages

Malaysia — Malay · 中文 · English, often mixed. A cross-cutting rule, never a “pick your language” quiz.
THE RULE

Match the task’s language for subject terms and required output, mirror the child’s comfortable language for rapport and scaffolding, default to English when uncertain, and treat code-switching as normal — not a mistake.

Three signals decide the language each turn

SignalWhat it isWhat it leads
task_languageLanguage / script of the problem itselfSubject terms & required output
learner_comfort_languageThe child’s stronger languageComprehension & scaffolding
turn_languageWhat the child used this turnMirrored for rapport

English is always the fallback. Policy, not a lock: adapt tone freely, but keep task terms and the answer language stable within a problem — don’t flip denominator / penyebut / 分母 every turn unless that term is the lesson.

Content subject — language is the medium

Math, science. Language should reduce load: the child may frame and reason in their stronger language even if the worksheet is in Malay — comprehension of the math is never blocked by language. Subject terms and the written answer still follow the task language.

Language subject — language is the lesson

BM karangan, English comprehension, Chinese 听写. The target language leads throughout; the child’s first language is allowed only for emotional safety and brief instructions — never to bypass target-language practice.

Per-step touchpoints

StepLanguage behavior
0 · Session OpenInfer / confirm the child’s comfort language from memory and first turns.
1 · Capture & ParseDetect problem language, script, type, and whether language is the subject.
2 · Frame the TaskApply the content-subject vs. language-subject rule.
4 · Step LoopEach turn carries its spoken language, term set, code-switch policy, and required output language.
5 · Answer CheckEnforce the required output language / terms for the task.
6 & 8 · MemoryPersist comfort language, code-switch pattern, term preferences, per-language signals.

Build notes

  • “Chinese” generates as Mandarin, Simplified — but detection accepts Traditional and mixed scripts rather than flagging them as errors.
  • Per-learner term glossary (denominator / penyebut / 分母) keeps terminology consistent across turns and sessions.
  • Safety, moderation, and escalation are multilingual — English-only guardrails are not enough.
  • STT, vision, verifier, and TTS all take language as input; the verifier accepts the child’s “why” in any of the three languages.
  • Verify: the current TTS voice (marin) is English-oriented — confirm natural Malay and Mandarin pronunciation before relying on it; per-language voices may be needed.

From process to system

How the workflow above becomes software the model can’t talk its way around.
THE PRINCIPLE

Treat the model as a fluent improviser inside a cage the system controls. The system owns the rhythm, the truth, the memory, and the boundaries; the model owns only the words. Whenever pedagogy depends on something being reliable, code decides it — not the prompt.

Six moves that turn the process into a system

Rhythm

Server-owned phase machine

A Durable Object per session owns the phase, the legal moves, and the wait/hint timer. The model is told the phase — it never picks it.

now: self-reported → authoritative FSM
Meaning

Comprehension gate

Three Reads + restatement become a state the session can’t exit. Solving moves stay forbidden until the child has framed the task.

now: a suggestion → a hard exit condition
Contract

Validated TutorAction

Every turn is a structured action — move, support level, one cognitive demand, language — validated against policy before it is spoken.

now: free utterance → checked schema
Truth

Separate verifier

A second call (symbolic where it can be) judges correctness — never the warm voice. Fixes the “confirm correct, miss the rest” trap.

now: self-graded → independent judge
Memory

Externalized learner model

Per-skill mastery, misconceptions, and summaries in D1, updated by plain functions — so the next session opens with what the child actually needs.

now: sessions are islands → durable continuity
Care

Behaviour & safety

Detect anxious / rushing / shutdown and adapt the strategy; moderate every output, hold homework integrity, and escalate distress to a guardian.

now: thin → multilingual guardrails

Build order — highest leverage first

01

Wrap the pipeline in a validated TutorAction — load real state; check phase, move, word count, no-answer, and safety before TTS. Smallest change, biggest payoff.

02

Add the comprehension gate — Three Reads + restatement before any solving move.

03

Introduce the SessionRuntime Durable Object — serialize turns and own the wait/hint timer. This is where the two-minute rule becomes real.

04

Add the separate verifier for answer checks.

05

Externalize the learner model + session summaries — the continuity users will feel.

06

Parameterize pedagogy as a versioned spec (phase × mode × learner), then add behaviour adaptation.

07

Safety pipeline + guardian visibility, and cage Realtime behind the same controller.

“Done” isn’t “helpful.” It’s teaching — measured by probing questions, productive struggle, and transfer, with evals for “never gives the answer,” “requires restatement,” “fades support,” and code-switched input.

Settled vs. still to validate

The structure is decided; the numbers are starting points.

Settled — the structure

  • The step sequence and the session/per-problem split
  • Gate as a hard invariant, adaptively passed
  • Problem-type branching for framing
  • Conditional prior-knowledge
  • The step-loop shape, with a support floor and park-it exit
  • Verifier on checkable claims only
  • Session/skill-level transfer as the mastery proof
  • The trilingual language rule — task language, mirror the child, default English, code-switching is normal

Validate with real children (7–12)

  • The exact wait-timer thresholds per age band
  • What counts as “enough reasoning” to advance at each phase
  • The adaptive forms of gate-proof for anxious vs. rushing learners
  • How aggressively support fades
  • TTS pronunciation quality for Malay and Mandarin (the current voice is English-oriented)
  • STT, vision, and the verifier on code-switched (Manglish / rojak) input

Instrument these and revise against observed sessions — they are not final values.
